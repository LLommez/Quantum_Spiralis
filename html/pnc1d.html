Particle in a Box

The particle in a box is a foundational concept in quantum mechanics. It explores a particle confined to move within a box with infinitely high potential barriers.

Energy Levels

In quantum mechanics, the energy of the particle in a box is quantized. The energy levels are determined by the formula:

Energy (En) = (n²h²)/(8mL²)

where:

  • n is the quantum number (1, 2, 3...)
  • h is Planck's constant
  • m is the mass of the particle
  • L is the length of the box

Position of the Particle

The probability of finding the particle at a position x within the box is determined by the wavefunction:

ψn(x) = √(2/L) * sin(nπx/L)

Probability Density

The probability density is given by the square of the wavefunction:

|ψn(x)|² = (2/L) * sin²(nπx/L)
