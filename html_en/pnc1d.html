Particle in a Box

The particle in a box is a foundational concept in quantum mechanics. It explores a particle confined to move within a box with infinitely high potential barriers.

Wavefunction

The wavefunction for a particle in a 1D box is given by:

\[ \psi_n(x) = \sqrt{\frac{2}{L}} \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \]

Normalization of the Wavefunction

The wavefunction must satisfy the normalization condition:

\[ \int_0^L |\psi_n(x)|^2 dx = 1 \]

Steps to Normalize:

  1. Substitute \(\psi_n(x)\) into the integral:

    \[ \int_0^L \left(A \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)\right)^2 dx \]

  2. Factor out constants:

    \[ {A²}\ \int_0^L \sin^2\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) dx \]

  3. Use the trigonometric identity:

    \[ \sin^2(\theta) = \frac{1 - \cos(2\theta)}{2} \]

    Substitute into the integral:

    \[ {A^2}\ \int_0^L \frac{1 - \cos\left(\frac{2n\pi x}{L}\right)}{2} dx \]

  4. Simplify the integral:

    \[ \frac{A^2}{2} \int_0^L 1 \, dx - \frac{1}{2} \int_0^L \cos\left(\frac{2n\pi x}{L}\right) dx \]

  5. Evaluate the integrals:

    The first integral is:

    \[ \int_0^L 1 \, dx = L \]

    The second integral is:

    \[ \int_0^L \cos\left(\frac{2n\pi x}{L}\right) dx = \frac{\sin\left(\frac{2n\pi x}{L}\right)}{\frac{2n\pi}{L}}\Big|_0^L = 0 \]

  6. Combine the results:

    \[ \frac{A²}{2} \cdot L = 1 \]

    \[ {A} = \sqrt{\frac{2}{L}} \]

    Thus, the wavefunction is normalized.

Energy Levels

In quantum mechanics, the energy of the particle in a box is quantized. The energy levels are determined by the formula:

\[ E_n = \frac{n^2 h^2}{8mL^2} \]

where:

  • n is the quantum number (1, 2, 3...)
  • h is Planck's constant
  • m is the mass of the particle
  • L is the length of the box

Position of the Particle

The probability of finding the particle at a position \(x\) is determined by:

\[ |\psi_n(x)|^2 = \frac{2}{L} \sin^2\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \]
